ℹ Pull Log Tips ▼
  • Use the banner tabs above the pull list to switch between Special, Standard, Beginner, and any other banners you've pulled on.
  • The @N number next to each 6★ or 5★ is the pity count — how many pulls it took to reach that item from the last one of that rarity.
  • Red pity numbers (6★ ≥50, 5★ ≥8) indicate you were in or near soft pity when you got it.
  • Use the filter buttons (All / 6★ / 5★ / 4★) to isolate high-rarity pulls quickly.
  • Pity counters reset to 0 every time you get a 6★ (for 6★ pity) or a 5★/6★ (for 5★ pity). The bars show your current streak since the last hit.
  • Records only go back 90 days — if your last 6★ was pulled before that window, your pity may display lower than the real value.

Data Notice
⚠
Pull history is limited to the last 90 days. The Endfield API only returns records within a 90-day rolling window. Pulls older than 90 days are excluded. Pity counters may be understated if your last 6★ was over 90 days ago.
Detailed Statistics
ℹ Reading the Statistics ▼
  • Total Headhunts counts all pulls across every banner within the 90-day window — use this as a gauge of overall activity, not lifetime spending.
  • Oroberyl Spent is estimated at 500 per pull. If you used HH Permits directly, the actual Oroberyl cost is 0, but the pull still counts here.
  • Spark Progress shows your position in the current 120-pull spark cycle. Each 120 pulls lets you select one featured operator directly.
  • Lucky 6★ Pulls counts 6★ operators you received in 40 or fewer pulls — well before soft pity begins at pull #65.
  • The 6★ Pull History at the bottom lists every 6★ in order of acquisition with its pity count. Green = lucky (≤15), red = soft/hard pity (≥65).
  • Stats are calculated from your loaded data only. If you haven't loaded data yet, all values will be zero or empty.
ℹ Per-Banner Breakdown Tips ▼
  • Each card represents one banner pool. Special Headhunting (purple) is where limited and rate-up operators appear.
  • Avg 6★ Pity is the mean pulls-per-6★ across all your 6★ hits on that banner. Lower is luckier — global average with soft pity is roughly 50–60.
  • The current pity bars (6★ / 5★) at the bottom of each card show how far you are into the next pity cycle right now.
  • The Standard banner (blue) has no rate-up — 6★ drops are chosen randomly from the standard pool.
  • Beginner banner (gold) has a guaranteed 6★ within 20 pulls and can only be done 20 times total.
  • If a banner shows 0 pulls, it means no records were returned for that pool within the 90-day window.

ℹ Resource Planner Tips ▼
  • 500 Oroberyl = 1 pull. 1 Origeometry = 75 Oroberyl. Enter your current stockpile and the tracker calculates total pulls automatically.
  • HH Permits are used directly as 1 pull each — no Oroberyl conversion needed.
  • Hard Pity (÷80) shows how many guaranteed 6★ operators you're carrying — every 80 pulls always yields at least one 6★.
  • Spark (÷120) tells you how many times you can guarantee-select a featured operator from the spark exchange shop.
  • The Pull Chance Calculator on the right uses your loaded Special HH pity automatically. If no data is loaded it defaults to 0.
  • Soft pity begins at pull #65 — each pull past that adds +5% to the 6★ rate. Pull #80 is a guaranteed 6★ (hard pity).
  • The Featured 6★ probability assumes a 50/50 chance that a 6★ is the featured operator (standard 50/50 mechanic).

ℹ Income Forecast Tips ▼
  • Set your From date to today and your To date to the banner's estimated end date to see exactly how much you can farm in time.
  • Daily Missions cap at 200 Oroberyl/day. The Monthly Pass adds another 200/day if you're subscribed.
  • Weekly Routine rewards vary — 200 is a conservative baseline, 400 is the typical high-end if you clear all content.
  • Protocol Pass is the battle-pass-style reward track; ~150/week is a common estimate for free-to-play progression.
  • Event Oroberyl is spread across the patch cycle (~6 weeks / 42 days). Adjust upward for major events or downward for lighter patches.
  • The forecast does not include one-time rewards, login bonuses, or compensation drops — add those manually to your Oroberyl count in the Resource Planner.
  • Divide the projected Oroberyl total by 500 to get pull count, or read the "≈ N pulls" line in the result.
